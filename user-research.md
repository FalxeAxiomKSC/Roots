# User Research: Sustainability Funding Surfacing Interface

## Understanding the User: Cooperatives

### Who are Cooperatives?
- **Member-owned organizations** that operate for the benefit of their members
- **Democratic governance** - one member, one vote
- **Purpose-driven** - social, environmental, and economic goals alongside financial sustainability
- **Community-focused** - local impact and community benefit
- **Range of types**: housing, worker, consumer, agricultural, energy, and service cooperatives

### Key Characteristics of Cooperative Users
- **Mission-driven**: Value alignment is crucial when seeking funding
- **Community-oriented**: Decisions often involve group consensus
- **Resource-constrained**: Limited access to traditional capital markets
- **Impact-focused**: Need to demonstrate social/environmental impact
- **Transparency-demanding**: Open book accounting and reporting

## User Research Questions

### Primary User Goals
1. **Find appropriate funding** that aligns with cooperative values and mission
2. **Streamline application processes** to reduce administrative burden
3. **Build relationships** with funders who understand cooperative model
4. **Track application progress** and manage multiple funding opportunities
5. **Demonstrate impact** to secure future funding

### Pain Points & Challenges
1. **Complex funding landscape** - Many different types of funding with varying requirements
2. **Time-consuming applications** - Lengthy, repetitive processes across multiple funders
3. **Misalignment of values** - Traditional funders may not understand cooperative principles
4. **Limited capacity** - Small teams with multiple responsibilities
5. **Information overload** - Difficulty finding relevant opportunities
6. **Unclear criteria** - Vague guidelines and inconsistent evaluation processes

### Funding Journey Map

**Phase 1: Discovery & Awareness**
- Need: Find available funding opportunities
- Pain: Don't know where to start, information scattered
- Solution: Curated opportunity matching based on cooperative profile

**Phase 2: Research & Evaluation**
- Need: Understand eligibility and requirements
- Pain: Complex terms, unclear criteria, time-consuming research
- Solution: Clear eligibility indicators, simplified comparison tools

**Phase 3: Application & Preparation**
- Need: Submit compelling applications efficiently
- Pain: Repetitive forms, missing documentation, deadline pressure
- Solution: Smart forms, document management, deadline tracking

**Phase 4: Follow-up & Management**
- Need: Track applications and build relationships
- Pain: Lack of transparency, poor communication, multiple platforms
- Solution: Centralized dashboard, communication tools, progress tracking

## Cooperative-specific Considerations

### Value Alignment Needs
- **Social impact metrics** beyond financial returns
- **Environmental sustainability** indicators
- **Community benefit** measurements
- **Democratic governance** documentation
- **Member equity** and fair labor practices

### Technical Requirements
- **Accessibility** for diverse membership
- **Mobile-friendly** for field work
- **Multi-language** support for diverse communities
- **Data privacy** protection for member information
- **Offline capabilities** for rural areas

### Emotional Context
- **Hope** for creating positive change
- **Frustration** with bureaucratic barriers
- **Determination** to overcome challenges
- **Collaborative mindset** - working together
- **Patience** with slow-moving processes
